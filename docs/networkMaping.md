Below is the use of network making in Garuda NDPS:

A. The Station House Officer (Normal SHO)
Local Source Tracing: When local officers arrest a consumer, they can add the suspect's direct supplier (peddler) to the system. The graph immediately maps this link.
Cross-Station Alerts: If a peddler operating in Station A is buying contraband from a supplier registered in neighboring Station B, the graph shows this link. The SHO of Station A can instantly coordinate with the SHO of Station B to conduct a joint raid.
Targeting Local Hubs: By looking at the size of the nodes (which scales based on connectivity), the SHO can see which local peddler is responsible for supplying the most consumers in their jurisdiction, prioritizing their arrest.
B. The Cyber SHO (Cyber Surveillance & Analytics Unit)
Digital Footprint Correlation: Cyber cells deal with abstract data—CDR (Call Detail Records), IMEI logs, and UPI transaction IDs. The chain builder correlates these: if two offenders from different stations share an active IMEI or have transacted with the same UPI ID, the system automatically creates a connection.
Identifying the "Shadow" Kingpins: Top-level drug coordinators (Kingpins/Financiers) rarely touch physical contraband, making them hard to convict. However, the Cyber SHO can use PageRank and Centrality analytics built into this graph to find nodes that act as "financial hubs" or logistically connect multiple distant peddler groups.
Supply Chain Disruption: By tracing links across multiple hops (e.g., Consumer -> Peddler -> Transporter -> Kingpin), the Cyber SHO can map the path of synthetic drugs entering the district, tracing the digital trail back to the source.
C. The Superintendent of Police (SP) and SDPOs (Command Level)
Top-down Intervention: Instead of playing "whack-a-mole" by making dozens of minor consumer arrests (which does not slow down the trade), leadership can look at the network to identify the core suppliers. Targeting a single highly connected supplier can collapse five or six active peddling groups simultaneously.
District-wide Resource Allocation: The SP can view the clusters and filter by contraband type (e.g., Ganja vs. MDMA) to see which divisions are hubs for specific narcotics, allowing them to dispatch specialized task forces (like the Narcotics Task Force) where they will have the greatest impact.
