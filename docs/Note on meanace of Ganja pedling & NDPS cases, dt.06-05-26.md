## **I. BACKGROUND** 

Tirupati District has been witnessing a persistent threat from ganja peddling and other NDPS related offences, which are adversely affecting public order, youth, students, workers, pilgrims, and the overall law and order environment in the district. The illegal trafficking and consumption of ganja has gradually evolved into an organized criminal activity involving interstate suppliers, local peddlers, consumers, transporters, financiers, and habitual offenders. 

Analysis of crime data reveals that during the last 10 years, a total of 358 NDPS cases were registered in Tirupati District against 1323 accused persons. Out of them, 1187 accused were arrested, while 116 accused are still absconding and yet to be arrested. The data clearly indicates the existence of an extensive network of peddlers and habitual offenders operating within the district.

The growing availability of ganja among youth and vulnerable sections is leading to increased addiction, property offences, violent crimes, financial distress, and other social problems. Hence, continuous 

surveillance, intelligence collection, preventive policing, and strict enforcement are essential for effective control of NDPS related activities. 

The problem is no longer limited to isolated peddling. It has evolved into a structured illegal network involving: 

- Interstate supply chains 

- Local peddlers and habitual offenders 

- Student and youth targeting 

- Courier/parcel-based transportation 

- Concealed storage in rented premises and lodges 

- Use of digital payments and mobile communication 

- Repeated involvement of known offenders and criminal associates 

This has resulted in rising ganja consumption among youth and students.   Increased petty crimes, thefts, violence, and addiction-linked offences, organized criminal activity, interstate drug movement, threat to public order, educational institutions, and pilgrim safety. 

In view of the above, Tirupati District Police shall adopt a multidimensional Anti-Ganja Strategy integrating preventive policing, intelligence-led enforcement, technical surveillance, offender profiling, predictive policing, and digital monitoring to identify, disrupt, and dismantle ganja networks in the district.
